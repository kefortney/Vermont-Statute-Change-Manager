Sec. 1.  18 V.S.A. § 4812 is added to read: 14 
§ 4812.  RECOVERY RESIDENCE CERTIFICATION 15 
(a)  Definition.  As used in this section, “recovery residence” means a 16 
shared temporary living residence supporting persons recovering from a 17 
substance use disorder that provides care through peer support and assistance 18 
accessing support services and community resources available to persons 19 
recovering from substance use disorders. 20

(b)  Certification.  Upon receipt of an application from a recovery residence 1 
in the State seeking certification, the Department shall issue the requested 2 
certification if it determines that the applicant meets the following minimum 3 
standards: 4 
(1)  the applicant is certified by either a Vermont affiliate of the National 5 
Alliance for Recovery Residences or another organization approved by the 6 
Department;  7 
(2)  the applicant demonstrates the capacity to operate a recovery 8 
residence in accordance with rules adopted by the Department of Health and in 9 
a manner that ensures person-centered care and resident dignity; 10 
(3)  the applicant shall fully comply with standards for health, safety, and 11 
sanitation as required by State law, including standards set forth by the State 12 
Fire Marshal, the Department, and municipal ordinance; 13 
(4)  once certified, the recovery residence, including the buildings and 14 
grounds, shall be subject to inspections by the Department with a ten-day prior 15 
notice; 16 
(5)  the applicant shall have a clear process for responding to resident 17 
complaints, including: 18 
(A)  a method by which each resident shall be made aware of the 19 
complaint procedure; 20
